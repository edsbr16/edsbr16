# Hi, I'm Erica 

Computer Science undergraduate at Durham University

---

I've always been drawn to the moment when something hidden becomes visible —
when scattered pieces suddenly form a pattern, and you think: *oh, that's what was going on.*

That feeling is what connects everything I do.

---

### The through-line

I watch crime documentaries the same way I approach a PCAP file: as a puzzle about *people*.
What did they do, when, and why — and what trace did they leave behind without realising?
That's what pulled me into cybersecurity, specifically forensics and intelligence work.
Not just the technical side, but the human story underneath it.

It's also why I love games. The best ones don't just give you a challenge —
they give you a world with internal logic, and trust you to figure it out.
I think a lot about what it would look like to build that kind of experience
around security and adversarial thinking. A puzzle game where the player is the analyst.
A world where staying safe online doesn't feel like a lecture — it feels like a mystery to solve.

And underneath all of it: I just really love making things.
Code, yes — but also paintings, embroidery, crocheted objects, things built in Blender.
There's something about taking raw material and shaping it into something
that has never existed before that I find genuinely hard to explain and impossible to stop doing.
